Reflections

Insights from Client Meetings

Our discussions with the Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens revealed crucial insights that shaped our entire approach.

Privacy and Discretion: They value discretion and privacy more than flashy tech. This insight led us to prioritize anonymous counting methods and reject any camera-based solutions that might make visitors uncomfortable.

Heritage Sensitivity: Budget and listed building status drive every decision. We learned that even the most innovative solution is worthless if it can't be implemented within the strict heritage guidelines that protect the Kibble Palace's historical integrity.

What We Learned from Prototyping

The prototyping process revealed both technical challenges and design opportunities we hadn't initially considered.

Directional Accuracy: Our initial solution lacked the capability to track direction of footfall, resulting in high inaccuracy. This discovery led us to develop the dual-sensor approach that became central to our final design.

Real-world Constraints: Sketching the flow revealed practical constraints (e.g., group entries, line-of-sight) that we hadn't anticipated in our initial concepts.

Rapid Iteration: Paper prototyping allowed for quick sketches to be made, avoiding costly mistakes in the digital implementation phase. This approach helped us test multiple concepts quickly and identify the most promising directions.
