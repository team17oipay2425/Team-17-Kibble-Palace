Week 1

Day 1 - Stakeholder Meeting

For our stakeholder meeting, we met with Chrissy from Friends of Glasgow Bontanic Gardens for insights on footfall tracking in the gardens. She explained the daily operational challenges: with staff struggling to justify resource allocation without concrete visitor data and a distinct lack of usage statistics resulting in weakening funding applications. Through this meeting, we now had an insight on the challenges of accurate footfall counting in a listed building with limited budget and strict modification rules.

Key insights from the meeting: Any solution must be invisible to visitors, along with no structural modifications to the building. Budget constraints are also a significant factor, as well as ensuring that the plants remain unaffected.

Day 2 - Initial Research

After the stakeholder meeting, we begun exploring existing solutions and their limitations. An extensive brainstorming session was carried out where we explored multiple technical approaches. Some suggestions were initially considered, such as thermal imaging or pressure plates, but were rejected eventually due to privacy concerns and cost. More advanced solutions were also considered, such as using computer vision, but were rejected due to the complexity of implementation and the need for significant computational resources.

We eventually settled on a IR sensor approach, which would be cost-effective and easy to install without modifying the building structure. The system would consist of two IR sensors placed at the entrance, counting entries and exits while remaining discreet.

Day 3 - Ideation Sketches

After designing on our solution, we started out by creating a lo-fi prototype of a IR sensor system. During the process, we realised placement and directionality matter more than expected, as single sensors would overcount due to multiple people moving back and forth. This led to the conclusion that a dual sensor setup would be necessary to accurately count foot traffic. We also sketched potential dashboard layouts and discussed data visualization requirements.

Week 2

Day 5 - Dashboard Prototyping

We focused on designing the dashboard interface for the staff use. Based on the client meeting and understanding of their needs, we prioritized features like real-time visitor counts, daily patterns, and historical data visualization. We created wireframes for different dashboard views: real-time occupancy display showing current visitor counts, daily summary charts tracking hourly patterns, weekly trend analysis for operational planning, and monthly reports suitable for council presentations and funding applications.

We designed the interface to show key metrics at a glance, as well as specialized sections for deeper insights: visitor trends showing daily patterns, coutry of origin data, as well as event tracking with attendance figures, helping staff to understand which programs drive the most engagement

At first, there was some struggles with balancing comprehensive data presentation while still retaining visual clarity. We eventually settled on a clean, spacious layout with large fonts and intuitive color coding, making it easier for staff to digest the information quickly which still providing the detailed analytics needed for strategic planning and reporting.

Day 6 - Interviewing Visitors of Kibble Palace

Today, we visited Kibble Palace to gather feedback from actual visitors about their awareness and thoughts on footfall tracking. Surprisingly, many assumed that there was already a tracking system in place and were unaware that the data was oudated.

Most visitors were open to the idea of anonymous tracking if it can help improve the management of the place, especially if it preserves the free entry policy. We explained our concept (IR sensors + QR code form) and the feedback we got was posititve provided it remained non-intrusive and didn't feel like surveillance. However, they also highlighted that they would not be incentivised to scan the QR code form as it did not benefit their visit to the Kibble Palace.
